Report: act_report - We Rate Dogs Project¶¶

Intoduction¶

The contains insights collected from the wrangle project of the WeRateDogs dataset.

WeRateDogs is a Twitter account that rates people's dogs with a humorous comment about the dog. These ratings almost always have a denominator of 10. The numerators, though? Almost always greater than 10. 11/10, 12/10, 13/10, etc.

This archive contains basic tweet data (tweet ID, timestamp, text, etc.) for all 5000+ of their tweets as they stood on August 1, 2017. More on this soon.

Dataset was analyzed and visualized in this wrangling project

Insights¶

  1. Visualizing the tweet source with the most tweets. The visualization shows that most of the rating tweets were made from person using an iPhone device, followed by web users and least from users using a tweet deck

  1. Distribution of rating numerator. The visualization from the cleaned datset hihlights that majority of dog ratings fell between 0 and 20, howevere there were also outliers in this rating numbers, with dogs being rated as high as 166. Using a log transform shows no further insight as regards the distribution of this numerator rating

>

  1. Pupper dog breeds had the most rating count. However it is notable to mention that this finding is biased as only a fraction of the tweets in the cleaned data had dog breeds included. Out of the 1702 tweets from the cleaned dataset, only 350 tweets had dog breeds included

  1. Time series of the volume of tweet rating across the years. This time series shows the trend of participation by users in the dog rating community over the years. Rating tweets peaked around the early 2016 period and took a dive in the second quarter of the year. It continued to maintain a relatively simialar volume of participation into 2017

Conclusion¶

The Dog rating community had the most activity in early 2016, but the trend has since taken adive and only gets a relatively little interactions. Pupper is the most love dog breed, however this is subject to scrutiny as there is too little data to establish this. Most community participants are iPhone users, with most of them rating the dogs between 10 and 20.
